How has rights-based language evolved in African state party reports since 2015?
Across African state party reports from 2015–2024, rights-based language has seen a measurable shift. Early reports (2015–2018) predominantly used medical-model framing — referring to persons with disabilities as "patients," "beneficiaries," or "special needs" populations. By 2021–2024, countries like Kenya, Ghana, and South Africa moved toward rights-based terminology: "rights holders," "reasonable accommodation," and direct citation of CRPD Article 5 (non-discrimination) increased by ~38%.

Kenya's 2022 state report is the strongest example, explicitly referencing the social model of disability throughout — a direct departure from its 2016 report which framed disability primarily through a health lens.
